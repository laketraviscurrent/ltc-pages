The Lake Travis 4th of July Guide

Every fireworks show, hometown parade, and patio party worth your Saturday — mapped from the north shore to the Hill Country.

This year the celebration carries extra weight: 2026 marks America's 250th birthday, and the lake is leaning in. Lago Vista is running a "250 Years of American Spirit" parade, Lakeway's golden-era march rolls on, and the fireworks are stacked three nights deep. Whether you're anchoring out for Mack's show, claiming a curb in Lakeway, or just chasing the best patio view, here's your full guide to celebrating Independence Day Lake Travis-style.

On the Water Parades & Hometowns Festivals & Fireworks Plan Your Day
At a glance: Independence Day falls on Saturday, July 4, 2026. Fireworks run Thursday through Saturday this year — Volente lights up all three nights, Starnes Island goes off on the 3rd, and the big finales land on the 4th. Most parades and family events are free; on-water shows are best seen by boat.

On the Water

Lake Travis was built for this. Find a cove, drop anchor, and look up.

Free · By Boat

Gallery Furniture & Mattress Mack Fireworks Show

Big boats, bigger booms, and one seriously Texas-sized sky show on the north shore.

  • Fireworks at dusk, around 8:30pm
  • Launched between markers 15 & 16, north side of the lake
  • A choreographed 20-minute show by Big Dog Pyro

When: Sat, July 4 · 8:30pm  |  Where: Lake Travis, north side  |  Cost: Free

Free · By Boat

Starnes Island Fireworks

One of Central Texas' most iconic shows — hundreds of boats raft up around the island for the best seats on the water.

  • Fireworks typically around 9:30pm
  • Part of the Mattress Mack tradition between markers 15–16
  • Best by boat; shore viewing at Volente Beach Resort

When: Sat, July 4 · ~9:30pm  |  Where: Starnes Island, near Volente  |  Cost: Free

$10+ · 3 Nights

Volente Beach July 4th Weekend

Three days of waterpark, lakefront BBQ, live music, and fireworks every single night.

  • Waterpark opens 11am daily
  • Live music at 7pm
  • Fireworks around 8:30–9pm

When: July 2–4 · 11am–11pm  |  Where: Volente Beach Resort & Waterpark  |  Cost: From $10  |  Info

Plan Your Day on the Lake

Going by boat? The Mattress Mack and Starnes Island shows are best seen from the water — get to your cove well before dusk, anchor with room to spare, and keep your navigation lights on for the ride home.

Staying on land? Free parades (Lakeway, Lago Vista) fill curbs early, and the Hill Country Galleria lot tightens up by early evening. Bring chairs, water, and sunscreen — early July heat is no joke around here.

Heads up: The 4th lands on a Saturday in 2026, so expect a packed three-day weekend on the water and on 620. Build in extra drive time.
